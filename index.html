About

I am a theoretical physicist and string theorist from Dublin, Ireland.

My research is on E-theory which actively explores the unifying role of exceptional hidden symmetries, generated by Kac-Moody algebras such as $E_{11}$, as fundamental symmetries of M-theory, string theory, and gravity.

A list of my publications is given on arXiv, Google Scholar, and InspireHEP. A non-technical motivation for each work is provided in Research.

Recently I was a Postdoctoral Scholar at the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology (OIST) in Okinawa Japan. Before this, I obtained my PhD in Mathematics with the Theoretical Physics Group at King's College London (KCL) in 2023, under the supervision of Professor Peter West FRS.

As an undergraduate, I read Mathematics at Trinity College Dublin, graduating with a BA (Mod). I also hold an MSc in Theoretical Physics with Distinction from King's College London, where I was awarded the MSc Theoretical Physics Prize.

In September 2026 I will join the School of Mathematics at Trinity College Dublin as a Teaching Fellow, where I will continue my theoretical physics research alongside teaching and giving seminars on my work. I am currently a visitor in the School of Mathematics.

The courses I am teaching and research projects I am offering are listed in the Teaching & Research Projects tab above.
